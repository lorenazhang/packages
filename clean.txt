Why Conversion Rate?

Definition

Conversion Rate = % of customer calls that eventually become complaints

⸻

Key Finding

75% of conversions occur within 8 days

→ Indicates a predictable relationship between calls and complaints

⸻

Benefits

✔ Normalizes call volume across call drivers

✔ Filters out random call activity

✔ Potential early warning signal for emerging complaint trends


Limitations & Considerations

1. Call Drivers and Complaint Reasons Represent Different Dimensions

Call drivers capture the primary reason a customer contacted the bank, while complaint reasons capture the issue that ultimately resulted in customer dissatisfaction.

As a result, the relationship between call drivers and complaint reasons is not always one-to-one. Certain complaint reasons—particularly experience-related categories such as Employee Behavior, Employee Knowledge, and Wait Time / Multiple Transfers—may span multiple call drivers and therefore may not exhibit a clear conversion pattern at the call-driver level.

⸻

2. Conversion Rate Depends on Upstream Call Driver Classification

Call drivers are generated by an NLP classification model rather than manually assigned by customers or agents.

Therefore, the effectiveness of the conversion rate signal is dependent on the quality of the upstream model. The current call driver model has an estimated accuracy of approximately 68%, which may introduce classification noise and impact the strength of observed conversion patterns.



Conversion rate should be viewed as a directional signal rather than a definitive measure, and should be interpreted alongside complaint trends, complaint summaries, and other supporting indicators.
